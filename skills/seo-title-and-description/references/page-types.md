# Page-Type Directions

Use the closest page type after reviewing both the target page and current SERP. Choose the dominant pattern; do not combine every available angle.

## SaaS and online-tool priorities

| Page type | Typical intent | Useful Title directions | Useful Meta Description focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SaaS homepage | Branded or category discovery | Category and core outcome; use brand only when it is essential to the query | Audience, primary jobs, differentiation, next step |
| Product or feature page | Transactional or solution seeking | Primary capability plus outcome | What it does, who it helps, evidence-backed benefit |
| Free online tool | Immediate task completion | Task keyword, online or free only when true | Supported input/output, ease, speed, access conditions |
| Use-case page | Problem and solution fit | Use case plus audience or desired outcome | Workflow, pain point, result, relevant product capability |
| Industry or persona page | Vertical solution fit | Product category plus industry or role | Industry-specific need, use cases, credible benefit |
| Integration page | Navigational or transactional | Product A plus Product B integration | What connects, resulting workflow, setup expectation |
| Template page | Asset seeking | Template keyword, format, audience | What is included, editability, examples, access conditions |
| Pricing page | Commercial or transactional | Brand or product plus pricing or plans | Plan choice, transparent facts, trial or free plan only if true |
| Comparison or versus page | Commercial investigation | A vs B plus decision angle | Key comparison scope and who each option suits |
| Alternatives page | Commercial investigation | Product alternatives plus year only if maintained | Selection criteria, number of options, intended audience |
| Customer story or case study | Evidence seeking | Customer plus product and verified outcome | Problem, implementation, and substantiated result |

For an online tool, favor the user's immediate job over broad brand messaging. For a SaaS product page, connect the capability to a concrete outcome without inventing performance claims.

## Content-site priorities

| Page type | Typical intent | Useful Title directions | Useful Meta Description focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| How-to article | Informational task completion | How to plus task, supported qualifier | Steps, result, intended skill level, included aid |
| Listicle or best-of | Commercial or informational comparison | Honest number plus category and audience | Selection basis, use case, differentiators, freshness only if verified |
| Ultimate or pillar guide | Broad informational learning | Topic plus guide, checklist, or examples | Coverage, reader outcome, practical resources |
| Review | Commercial investigation | Product review plus decision qualifier | Tested scope, strengths and limits, intended buyer |
| Definition or glossary | Informational understanding | What is plus concept, or concise definition angle | Plain-language meaning, why it matters, useful examples |
| Statistics or research | Data seeking | Topic plus statistics, report, or study | Dataset scope, date, methodology, key value without unsupported numbers |
| Checklist or template article | Practical resource seeking | Task plus checklist or template | Included asset, steps, intended audience, access conditions |
| News or update | Fresh information | Specific event or change plus verified date | What changed, impact, source, affected audience |
| FAQ or question page | Informational answer | Exact question plus honest answer scope | Direct answer and supporting detail covered on the page |

Do not add the current year merely because competing Titles use it. Use it only when the page is genuinely current and maintained.

## Supporting page types

| Page type | Useful direction |
| --- | --- |
| Ecommerce product | Product/category, defining attribute, concise brand; describe verified specifications, availability, and benefit. |
| Ecommerce category | Category keyword, audience or defining selection; summarize range and shopping help without stuffing variants. |
| Local service or location | Service plus real location; describe service area, audience, and verified differentiator. |
| Marketplace or directory | Entity type plus real category or location; describe listing scope, filters, and verified coverage. |
| Documentation or help | Exact task, feature, or error; describe the resolution or configuration covered. |
| Course or event | Topic, format, audience, and verified date or credential; describe the learning or attendance outcome. |
| Jobs or careers | Role, team, and location or remote status; describe verified responsibilities and candidate fit. |
| Person or profile | Name plus verified role or expertise; describe identity, organization, and page purpose. |
| Login or account utility | Brand plus exact task; keep copy direct and avoid promotional clutter. |

## Candidate diversity

Across the five pairs, vary legitimate emphasis rather than swapping synonyms:

1. Best overall intent match and page value.
2. Primary task or outcome.
3. Audience, use case, or pain point.
4. Verified differentiator, format, or resource.
5. Trust or action angle; use a brand angle only when the brand is exceptionally well known or essential to navigational intent.

Keep every candidate faithful to the same page. If an angle needs a claim the page does not support, do not use that angle.

The Title brand rule in `SKILL.md` overrides any page-type suggestion: do not append a terminal brand or site-name suffix unless the strict exceptional-brand rule is satisfied. When a brand is necessary to identify a pricing, login, profile, or similar page, use it as the natural subject instead of a boilerplate suffix.
